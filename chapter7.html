Chapter 7 - Annuity Contracts

This lesson covers everything in depth - the annuity contract itself, application requirements, naming annuitant/beneficiary, funding sources, ongoing service, and the full claims process. Take your time and work through it like a textbook chapter.

📄 The annuity contract

Life and term annuities are insurance policies - legal contracts between client and insurer. General contract provisions apply across all policies; other terms are specific to the annuity type. Payout and accumulation annuity contracts differ significantly in structure.

Payout annuity finality: once issued, no changes can be made except changing the beneficiary. A free-look period MAY apply after issuance - the agent must tell the client whether the insurer offers one. After the free-look period ends, no rescission for a refund is possible.

💲 Annuity rate mechanics

If a quoted rate has no guarantee attached, income is calculated based on the annuity rate in effect on the date the single (or last of multiple) premium is actually received - not the date of the original illustration. This makes prompt funding important.

Amendments: can waive a provision or correct an error, but are valid only when prepared in writing by the insurer AND signed by the investor.

📝 The application process

Applications vary widely in format and wording between insurers - some are all-in-one, others require separate attached forms. The agent must complete the application together with the investor in one sitting - never take a partial application away to finish alone.

Error correction: printed form errors are corrected with BOTH investor and agent initialing the change near the correction. Online application errors are corrected by deleting and re-entering before submission - read with great care before transmitting. Neither agent nor investor may modify, cancel, or waive any application question - doing so can void the application.

📜 The reason-why letter for annuities

Prepared at or shortly after application submission. It paraphrases what the agent learned from the client, confirms what was discussed and when, sets out the plan of action, and substantiates why the annuity is suitable. It must be brief and easy to understand.

Important distinction: the reason-why letter does NOT confirm the policy has been issued - that's a separate event. The agent reviews it with the client, gets agreement, then keeps a copy for the client file and gives one to the client.

👥 Naming annuitant and beneficiary

Owner = annuitant required if registered or funded by registered savings. A joint annuity names an annuitant AND joint annuitant (co-annuitant) - the joint annuitant becomes sole annuitant on the first death; the beneficiary gets nothing until the SECOND annuitant dies, and only if a guarantee period exists.

No guarantee period = no beneficiary: if there's no guarantee period, a beneficiary cannot be named at all, then or later. Locked-in pension funding may make the death benefit payable to the spouse instead of a named beneficiary, unless a spousal waiver form is signed. Contingent beneficiaries are ALWAYS revocable (unlike primary beneficiaries, which can be either).

🔍 Owner identification exceptions

The same FINTRAC identification rules from Chapter 6 apply to annuity owners, with two specific exceptions where they do NOT apply: (1) when funded by a transfer from a registered pension plan or proceeds of a group life insurance policy, and (2) when the contract is registered.

Additional requirement: the owner must provide evidence of the age of each annuitant - critical since annuity calculations depend directly on age.

💰 Funding sources

Annuities can be funded from many sources: personal savings/chequing, bonds, GICs, TFSAs, stocks/mutual funds/segregated funds, RRSPs, RPP (DCPP or DBPP recognized participation amount), RRIFs, LIRAs, LIFs/LRIFs, DPSPs, PRIFs, and RLIFs.

Key distinction: a NEW deposit (e.g. fresh RRSP contribution) is tax-deductible at the time of deposit. A TRANSFER of existing registered savings (e.g. RRSP-to-annuity) is NOT a new contribution - no tax implications at the transfer itself, since the funds simply continue their existing tax-deferred status.

🤝 Ongoing service and contract delivery

Contracts aren't necessarily delivered in person - insurers may mail or courier them. In-person delivery (when possible) lets the agent reinforce value and answer questions. Once delivered, servicing centers on keeping beneficiary designations accurate and, for accumulation annuities, managing deposits and maturity decisions.

Beneficiary changes: use a change of beneficiary form, signed by the owner. Once submitted and processed, ALL previous designations are revoked. The completed form must be sent to the insurer immediately - changes are effective on signing, but the insurer won't honour them until written notice is RECEIVED.

💵 Handling deposits

Deposits can come as cash, cheque, or bank draft - all must be handled securely and forwarded to the insurer without delay. Cash deposits may trigger anti-money-laundering (PCMLTFA) obligations the agent must follow closely. The agent must provide a receipt for any cash received.

Security practice: cheques/drafts are made payable to the insurer (with the policy number noted if assigned). Never leave a deposit unattended - e.g. in a briefcase in an unoccupied vehicle - and store it under lock and key in the office, even briefly.

⚖️ The claims process

A claim form must be COMPLETE and include the claimant's signature before processing begins. The agent may assist with preparation, but once submitted to the insurer, a claims representative takes over - the agent is no longer involved in issuing cheques or transferring funds.

No living beneficiary: the claim falls to the owner, or if the owner is deceased, the estate - this means PROBATE FEES may apply, unlike when a named beneficiary receives proceeds directly (which bypasses probate).

🔁 Surrender, withdrawal, and POA claims

Accumulation annuities permit both withdrawals (subject to min/max) and surrender at any time. Term annuities can also be surrendered. A life annuity CANNOT be surrendered once payments have started.

MVA on early surrender: cashing in before maturity can trigger a market value adjustment based on remaining term, original interest rate, current rate, and fees - this reduces interest earned on the cashed-in amount. A surrender/withdrawal penalty may ALSO apply separately according to contract terms. POA claims require a special form; verify the POA's validity and scope, and seek legal opinion given provincial differences.

💸 Factors affecting the claim amount

Taxes are always withheld per the Income Tax Act when a contract is surrendered or a withdrawal is made. The actual amount received depends on whether a cash refund or instalment refund was chosen to protect capital, and whether a guarantee period exists.

Guarantee period math: a claim cannot be made against a life annuity unless a guarantee exists. The beneficiary is entitled to the portion not yet paid between the start of payments and the end of the guarantee (or the difference between premium and payments made to date) - with no further claim once the guarantee period ends, even if the annuitant's actual death occurs much later in real terms relative to the original guarantee window.
