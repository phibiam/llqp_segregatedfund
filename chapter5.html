Chapter 5 - Recommendations

Read this lesson first. It covers all key concepts you will be tested on.

✅ The recommendation process

Every recommendation must follow this sequence: Complete KYC, analyze needs, research suitable products, make and document the recommendation, deliver required disclosures (Fund Facts), then monitor ongoing suitability.

Core principle - Suitability: The recommended product must genuinely fit THIS client specific situation, goals and risk tolerance. Not just any client. THIS client, right now.

⚠️ Common suitability failure scenarios

Recommending a 10-year maturity seg fund to a 78-year-old client.

Recommending a high-risk equity fund to a conservative retiree needing income.

Transferring funds between products without documenting that it is in the client best interest - this is called churning.

Failing to update KYC after major life events such as divorce, retirement or job loss.

Documentation is your protection: If it is not written down it did not happen. Every recommendation, every client conversation and every KYC update must be documented.

📄 Key disclosure requirements

Fund Facts - Must be delivered BEFORE or AT the point of sale. Not after. Covers performance, costs, risks and guarantees.

Information folder - Delivered at or before contract issuance. Contains key contract features and Fund Facts for each selected fund.

Right of rescission - 2 business days from receiving the confirmation statement. Must be disclosed to every client.

Annual review - Mandatory ongoing service. Covers updated KYC, fund performance, beneficiary designations and whether the product still suits the client changed situation.
