Chapter 7 - Annuity Contracts

Read this lesson first. It covers all key concepts you will be tested on.

📄 The annuity contract process

Step 1 Complete KYC. Step 2 Shop annuity rates from multiple insurers. Step 3 Make recommendation. Step 4 Complete application - ALL elections are permanent once income starts. Step 5 Fund the contract directly. Step 6 Deliver contract to client. Step 7 Confirm all terms are correct. Step 8 Ongoing service.

Critical: Unlike seg funds, payout annuity terms are IRREVOCABLE once income begins. All elections (guarantee period, joint life, indexing, payment frequency) must be correct at application. There is no fix it later option.

⚠️ Key compliance rules for annuities

Registered transfers - Always direct transfer from RRSP or RRIF to avoid immediate taxation.

Locked-in funds - LIRA and LIF funds can only purchase a life annuity not a term certain annuity.

Successor annuitant - Must be a spouse; enables tax-free rollover at death of registered annuity holder.

AML obligations - Lump-sum annuity purchases require full identity verification. Cash over $10,000 requires FINTRAC report. Suspicious transactions require an STR regardless of amount.

Market value adjustment - Always explain MVA before recommending accumulation annuities. Rising rates reduce surrender value; falling rates increase it.

Beneficiary changes - Verify revocable vs irrevocable first - same as seg funds.

💸 Taxation summary for annuity contracts

Annuity typeTax treatment of payments
Registered annuity (RRSP or RRIF funded)100% taxable as ordinary income - no return of capital
Non-registered prescribed annuityBlend of non-taxable capital and taxable interest - same amount each year
Non-registered accrual annuityInterest front-loaded in early years - heavier tax early lighter later
Inherited payments from registered annuity100% taxable as ordinary income to the beneficiary each year
Registered annuity surrenderedFull surrender value taxable as income in the year received
