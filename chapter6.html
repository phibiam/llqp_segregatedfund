Chapter 6 - Seg Fund Contracts

Read this lesson first. It covers all key concepts you will be tested on.

📄 The seg fund contract process

Step 1 Complete KYC. Step 2 Make recommendation. Step 3 Deliver information folder and Fund Facts. Step 4 Complete application. Step 5 Submit to insurer. Step 6 Contract issued. Step 7 Deliver contract to client. Step 8 Confirm right of rescission which is 2 business days from receiving confirmation. Step 9 Post-sale follow-up. Step 10 Ongoing monitoring and annual review.

AML requirement: Cash transactions over $10,000 must be reported to FINTRAC. Verify client identity for all new accounts. File a Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) for any suspicious activity regardless of dollar amount.

⚠️ Key compliance rules

Never commingle funds - Client cheques must be payable to the insurer never the advisor.

Beneficiary changes - Always verify revocable vs irrevocable before processing. Irrevocable beneficiary requires their written consent to change.

POA transactions - Verify document is valid, enduring, covers insurance and has not been revoked. Keep a certified copy on file.

Fund switches - Provide Fund Facts for new fund; document rationale; note tax implications in non-registered accounts.

Resets - Always disclose the extended maturity date; get client acknowledgment in writing.

Withdrawals - Explain proportional guarantee reduction and any tax implications before processing.

📊 Claims and maturity

Death benefit claim: Notify insurer promptly. Obtain claim form. Gather certified death certificate and beneficiary ID. Submit complete package. Benefit paid directly to beneficiary outside the estate and probate-free if beneficiary is named.

At maturity: Client receives greater of market value or guaranteed amount. Options: renew for a new term (restarts the clock); convert to annuity; take lump sum.

Early surrender: Client receives market value minus any DSC fee. Maturity guarantee is FORFEITED. Advisors must explain both costs before processing.
