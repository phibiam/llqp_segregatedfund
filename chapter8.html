⚠️ Key group plan concepts

Vesting - When employer contributions become the employee property. Federal minimum: 2 years. Termination accelerates full vesting immediately for all members regardless of time served.

Pension Adjustment (PA) - Reduces RRSP contribution room for members of DBPPs, DCPPs and DPSPs to prevent double tax sheltering.

Locked-in accounts - When an employee leaves a DBPP or DCPP: savings go to a LIRA (savings phase) which is later converted to a LIF (income phase). Cannot be accessed before retirement age except in very limited circumstances.

DBPP underfunding - When plan assets cannot cover promised benefits. Sponsor must make special payments to restore funding within regulatory timelines.

📋 DBPP vs DCPP - the key distinction

DBPP formula: Annual pension equals 2% times years of service times final average salary.

DCPP: No pension formula. Retirement income equals whatever the accumulated contributions and investment returns produce.

Who bears investment risk? DBPP = Employer bears it. DCPP = Employee or member bears it. This is the most fundamental difference and the most commonly tested exam concept in Chapter 8.

🎓 Advisor obligations for group plans

Annual review with plan sponsor - Covers investment performance, participation rates, contribution adequacy, regulatory updates, plan design changes and member education needs.

Member education - Advisors have a duty to help members understand their investment options. Low member engagement signals a need for education programs.

Prompt remittance - Employer must remit employee contributions promptly after payroll. Late remittance is a fiduciary breach. Advisors must escalate if the issue persists.

Plan termination - Must follow pension legislation. All members immediately fully vested. Assets distributed via LIRA transfers or other prescribed options.
