Chapter 2 — Segregated Funds

Segregated funds are one of the most tested topics on the LLQP. This tutorial covers all the key concepts before you quiz yourself.

🏦 What is a segregated fund?

A segregated fund is an insurance contract — not a mutual fund. It is issued by a life insurance company and lets clients invest in a pool of assets while receiving insurance guarantees. Only LLQP-licensed advisors can sell them.

The assets are kept "segregated" (separate) from the insurance company's general assets — so if the insurer goes bankrupt, your client's money is protected.

Key difference from mutual funds: Seg funds offer maturity guarantees, death benefit guarantees, potential creditor protection, and bypass of probate. Mutual funds offer none of these.

🛡️ The two core guarantees

Maturity guarantee (75% or 100%): At the contract maturity date, the client receives the greater of the market value OR the guaranteed percentage of deposits. Example: $100,000 deposited with 75% guarantee = minimum $75,000 at maturity regardless of market performance.

Death benefit guarantee (75% or 100%): If the annuitant dies during the contract, the beneficiary receives the greater of market value OR the guaranteed percentage of deposits.

Top-up: If market value is below the guarantee, the insurer pays the difference. This is called a "top-up." Example: Market value = $60,000, guarantee = $75,000 → insurer pays $15,000 top-up.

🔄 Reset — locking in gains

If the fund grows in value, the client can "reset" their guarantee to the new higher value. This locks in market gains for guarantee purposes.

Important: Resetting also restarts the maturity date clock. A reset on a 10-year contract restarts the 10 years from the reset date. Advisors must disclose this before processing a reset.

🏛️ Probate bypass & creditor protection

Probate bypass: When a named beneficiary (not the estate) is on the contract, the death benefit passes directly to that person — bypassing the estate entirely. No probate fees, no delays, private transfer.

Creditor protection: When a preferred beneficiary class is named (spouse, child, parent, grandchild), the funds may be protected from creditor claims — even while the client is alive. Irrevocable designations provide the strongest protection.

🏢 Assuris protection

Assuris protects policyholders if the life insurance company issuing the seg fund becomes insolvent. It guarantees the greater of $100,000 or 90% of the guaranteed amount. It does NOT protect against market losses — only insurer failure.

⚠️ Key rules to remember

• Withdrawals before maturity forfeit the maturity guarantee — client gets market value only

• Withdrawals reduce the guarantee base proportionally (not dollar-for-dollar)

• No medical underwriting required for seg fund contracts

• Right of rescission: client can cancel within 2 business days of receiving confirmation

• Fund Facts must be delivered BEFORE or AT the point of sale — never after

• Cheques must be payable to the insurance company, never the advisor personally
