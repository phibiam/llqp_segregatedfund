Chapter 4 - Investor Profile

Read this lesson first. It covers all key concepts you will be tested on.

👤 What is the investor profile?

The investor profile is a comprehensive picture of a client financial situation, personal factors, needs and risk tolerance. It is the foundation of every recommendation. Without a complete profile no recommendation can be considered suitable.

Key documents reviewed: Income tax return (NOA), RRSP and RRIF statements, non-registered investment statements, pension plan statements, bank statements, life insurance policies, mortgage and credit card statements.

🏛️ Government retirement income

OAS: Available at 65. Can defer to 70 for 0.6% increase per month - max 36% more. NOT contribution-based. Clawback: 15% of income above approximately $90,000 threshold.

CPP: Contribution-based. Standard age 65. Start early at 60: reduced by 0.6% per month (max minus 36%). Defer to 70: increased by 0.7% per month (max plus 42%).

GIS: Non-taxable benefit for low-income OAS recipients. RRSP and RRIF withdrawals can REDUCE GIS entitlement. TFSA withdrawals do NOT affect GIS.

⚖️ Risk tolerance vs risk capacity

Risk tolerance - emotional willingness to accept losses. Subjective. Investors consistently OVERESTIMATE their own risk tolerance.

Risk capacity - financial ability to absorb losses without affecting lifestyle or goals. Objective.

Key principle: When in doubt lean toward the more conservative assessment. A client who says medium-high but would panic and sell in a 20% decline is NOT truly medium-high tolerance. Assess both separately.

🏢 Employer pension plans

DBPP - Defined Benefit. Employer promises a specific formula-based pension. Employer bears investment risk. Gold standard for employees.

DCPP - Defined Contribution. Contributions are defined but pension depends on investment performance. Employee bears investment risk. Assets are LOCKED-IN.

GRRSP - Group RRSP. NOT locked-in. Uses employee personal RRSP contribution room.

DPSP - Deferred Profit Sharing Plan. Employer contributions only. NOT locked-in. Creates a pension adjustment reducing RRSP room.

Pension Adjustment (PA): Reduces RRSP contribution room for members of DBPPs, DCPPs and DPSPs. RRSP room formula: 18% of prior year income minus PA.
