Chapter 3 - Annuities

Read this lesson first. It covers all key concepts you will be tested on.

💰 What is an annuity?

An annuity converts a lump sum into regular income payments. You give the insurance company your capital and they pay you back over time partly as return of capital and partly as interest.

Think of it as the opposite of life insurance: life insurance protects against dying too early; a life annuity protects against living too long.

Two core types: Payout annuities (pay regular income) and Accumulation annuities (save and grow, also called insurance GICs).

📋 Types of payout annuities

TypeHow long it paysBest for

Immediate vs deferred: Immediate annuity starts payments right away. Deferred annuity starts payments at a future date with an accumulation phase first.

💸 Taxation of annuities

Registered annuity (funded from RRSP or RRIF) - Every payment is 100% taxable as ordinary income. No return-of-capital component.

Non-registered prescribed annuity - Each payment blends capital (non-taxable) and interest (taxable). The SAME taxable amount every year. Predictable tax burden.

Non-registered accrual annuity - Interest is front-loaded in early years. More tax early, less later. Less tax-efficient than prescribed.

Prescribed requirements: Must not be indexed. Annuitant, payee and owner must be the same person. Payments must begin by Dec 31 of the year following purchase.

⚠️ Key risks and Assuris protection

Interest rate risk - Rates locked in at purchase forever. Cannot renegotiate. Managed by laddering annuity purchases over time.

Inflation risk - Fixed payment buys less every year. Managed with indexed annuities.

Liquidity risk - Once a payout annuity starts you generally cannot surrender it.

Loss of capital - Life annuity without guarantee: if you die early remaining capital stays with the insurer.

Assuris protection for annuities: Payout annuities - monthly payments up to $5,000 fully protected; above $5,000 protected at greater of $5,000 or 90%. Accumulation annuities - 100% up to $100,000 or 90% whichever is higher.

🆕 New annuity types

ALDA - Purchased with registered funds. Payments deferred until age 85. Max 25% of qualifying plan or $180,000 in 2025. Cannot be surrendered or provide guarantees. Excluded from RRIF minimum withdrawal calculations.

VPLA - For PRPP or DCPP members. Payments vary based on investment performance and group mortality. Minimum 10 retired members required. No guarantees.
